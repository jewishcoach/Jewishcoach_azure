# Process Map (S0–S15)

- **S0:** Contract – permission.
- **S1:** Topic – coaching focus (general only).
- **S2:** Event – specific conversation/interaction.
- **S3–S5:** Current state – emotion, thought, action (what you felt/thought/did in that moment).
- **S6:** Desired state – emotion, thought, action (what you would want to feel/think/do).
- **S7:** Gap.
- **S8:** Pattern.
- **S9:** Paradigm (thought behind the action).
- **S10:** Stance (reality perception) + trigger.
- **S11:** Gains and losses.
- **S12:** Forces (values and abilities).
- **S13:** Redesigning the desired (stance→paradigm→pattern + KaMaZ).
- **S14:** Vision.
- **S15:** Commitment.

## Process principle (Teshuva process / BSD)

- **Encounter and discovery:** Coaching is a shared encounter and discovery — not an exact science, and not an inquiry toward a **final definition** of “why” or “the precise cause.”
- **Pattern vs. changing reality:** A pattern is the **same inner response** (emotion–thought–action) recurring as **outer reality varies**. In the pattern stage (S8), you **do not** explain the pattern as caused by **relationship dynamics**, **the other person’s behavior**, or **expectations toward them** — those are **situation (surface)** layers; the core is **your response repeating**.
- **“Where” in the gap:** Around the gap stage, focus on **where you are inside the gap**, not on relational-causal analysis to “find the exact reason.”
- **Depth order:** After a summarized pattern → **paradigm** (S9: “that’s how it is for me” / thought-behind-action) → later, **stance and trigger** (S10). In S8 **do not** ask what “they cause” or “activates in them” — that framing pulls away from inner meeting and responsibility; external trigger appears **in S10** after paradigm and stance, not as a substitute for pattern work.

**Rule:** Do not skip stages. Advance only by gate.
