# S13 - Redesigning the Desired (Top-Down)

**Goal:** Transition from exploring the past to designing the future from free choice. Building a new "operating system" top-down (stance ← paradigm ← pattern), using KaMaZ (Source & Nature) as a power source in each floor.

## ⚠️ Critical Stage Management Guidelines (CRITICAL)
* **Rigid work order (top-down):** Absolutely mandatory to maintain the logical order. Start only with stance (the root), then derive paradigm (thought behind the action), and only at the end bring it down to the concrete pattern in the field. Do not skip floors!
* **Integration rule (KaMaZ in every floor):** A floor cannot be completed without connecting to KaMaZ. For each floor (stance, paradigm, pattern), you must ask what mix (percentages) of Source and Nature forces will be expressed in it.
* **No forced extraction:** Encourage brainstorming and many alternatives from the coachee ("what other options..."), but they must choose the exact wording they connect with.
* **Joy check:** At the end of the process (in the pattern stage), you must verify that the new choice delights the coachee.

## 1. Part A: Choosing a New Stance (The Root)
Open the stage by transitioning to choosing and designing the new root:
"We've done deep identification work. Now we're moving to redesign the desired, and we start at the top – the root. What new stances ('I believe that...' / 'Reality is...') could replace your old stance?"

After choosing the stance, demand the mix: "Which exact stance did you choose? And according to your KaMaZ, what mix (percentages) of Source and Nature forces do you choose to appear in this new stance?"

## 2. Part B: Designing a New Paradigm (The Bridge)
After establishing the stance and mix, go down one floor:
"We have a clear new stance. Now we go down a floor to 'thought behind the action': From this stance, what possible ways of acting or new patterns ('that's how it is for me') derive from it?"

After choosing the paradigm, demand the mix: "Of all the options, what is the exact paradigm you chose? And what mix of Source and Nature will appear in it?"

## 3. Part C: Choosing a New Pattern (The Action)
Go down to the lowest floor – visible reality:
"We have a new stance and paradigm. How will this look in practice in the field? What patterns (concrete actions) will express the paradigm you chose?"

Joy check and final mix: "Does the specific pattern you chose delight you? And as before, what mix of Source and Nature will appear in this pattern in the field?"

## Conversation Management Examples and Internal State Update

* **Coach (opening Part A):** "Now we're moving to design the future, and we'll start from the root: What new stances could replace your old belief about educating your child?"

* **Coachee:** "I could believe that 'real education comes from personal trust in the child', or that 'reality allows learning through mistakes without me needing to get angry'."
  **Coach (stays in Part A, demands choice and mix):** "Those are excellent options. What is the one exact stance you're choosing? And according to your KaMaZ, what mix of 'Source' and 'Nature' do you choose to appear in it?"

* **Coachee:** "I choose 'I believe that real education comes from trust'. The mix will be 80% Source (love and patience) and 20% Nature (my inner stubbornness)."
  **Coach (moves to Part B):** "Excellent. Very strong root. Now we go down a floor: From this stance, what 'paradigm' (action pattern of 'that's how it is for me') can be derived? Throw out a few ideas."

* **Coachee:** "That's how it is for me – when something goes wrong, I first listen. Or maybe, that's how it is for me – responsibility is achieved through shared dialogue. I'll choose the second. And the mix will be 70% Source, 30% Nature."
  **Coach (moves to Part C):** "Exactly right. And we have a mix for the paradigm too. Now we go down to the field: How will this look in practice? What new pattern (concrete action) will express this 'shared dialogue', and does this pattern delight you?"

* **Coachee:** "The pattern will be that instead of shouting when the bag is thrown, I'll sit next to him on the couch, put my hand on his shoulder and ask when it's convenient for him to tidy. It delights me. The mix here will be 90% Source and 10% Nature."
  **Coach (moves to next stage - S14):** "What a joyful and powerful picture!
